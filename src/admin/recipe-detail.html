Rawon

Main Course

Rawon is an Indonesian dish originating from Ponorogo in the form of black soup with a mixture of special spices using kluwek. Rawon, spread outside Ponorogo because Ponorogo has played an important role from time to time, so that it is known as a special dish from East Java (Arekan area), the people of Central Java to the east (Surakarta area). The meat for rawon is generally beef cut into small pieces, mainly the brisket.

How to make Rawon

1. Into a large pot, heat oil on medium-high heat.

2. Once oil is hot, add in the rawon spice mix, lime leaves, and lemongrass paste. Stir until mixture is fragrant.

3. Add in beef shank and toss it in the spice mixture until well-combined. Continue to cook until beef is seared.

4. Add in water, salt, pepper, tamarind paste, and granulated sugar. Turn down the heat to low-heat, and let simmer until the soup boils and meat is tender, about 1 hour.

5. Turn off the heat, then stir in chopped green onions.

6. Serve rawon over steamed rice, then garnish with your desired toppings.
